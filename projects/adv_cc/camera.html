Experimental Camera - Four Cuts of Life (Link)

Used

  • p5.js
  • ml5.js

Description

For this experimental camera, I decided to create one that represents the four stages of life: childhood, adulthood, elderhood, and death. I first got this idea from thinking about the photobooths that print out four pictures. In Korea, there is a popular photobooth called 인생네컷 (Four Cuts of Life) and I thought it would be interesting to visually interpret its name. For the layout, I decided to frame it on a film strip to make it feel like a timeline--a reel playing the story of a person's life, each frame capturing a distinct stage and emotion.

For childhood, I decided to make the camera heavily pixelated, like a retro video game, to evoke nostalgia and a playful, child-like energy. Also, I increased the brigtness to reflect innocence and purity. Finally, I made the camera leave a trail whenever you move to express how we don't really have much memory from our childhood; we only have blurry and fragmented ones.

For adulthood, I tried to represent the thrill and fleeting excitement of youth by making the camera evoke a nightclub atmosphere with flashing and changing colors.

For elderhood, I used cycling numbers to express the idea of running out of time. The flickering effect reinforces this, representing memory loss, instability, and the gradual decay of health.

Lastly, for death, I decided to cover the eyes to symbolize the loss of one's soul--drawing from the saying, 'the eyes are the windows to the soul.' For visual effects, I added a sepia tint and grainy texture to make it look like an old, fading photo. It reflect how, after death, a person gradually slips from memory, becoming part of the distant past.
